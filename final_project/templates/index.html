Onion Soup is a web application designed to collect, parse and plot article information from the Elsevier ScienceDirect database. It is designed to analyse the characteristics of the topical scientific sources immediately available to a university student with no specific clearance or entitlement to the Elsevier database. This application therefore allows to observe and analyse what a typical student seeking to inform him or herself is likely to be provided via elsevier. For any input field of research, Onion Soup collects a selection of available articles based on the ScienceDirect search engine as well as any available reference or suggested articles. It returns their metadata raw as well as in a series of relevant plots and graph.

Onion Soup is powered by the Elsevier ScienceDirect Search API, Selenium Webdriver, PhantomJS and BeautifulSoup. It assumes the public level of entitlement from a University Student and may only be used with a valid public University IP (e.g UChicago Library or another University's WiFi service)
